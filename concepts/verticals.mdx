---
title: 'Verticals'
description: 'Domain-specific benchmark slices that bias provider selection toward what works in your industry.'
---

`vertical` is part of every routing intent. It tells Speko which benchmark slice to score against.

| Vertical | Use for |
| --- | --- |
| `general` | Conversational AI, default catch-all. |
| `healthcare` | Clinical dictation, patient intake, telehealth. Boosts providers with strong medical terminology accuracy. |
| `finance` | Banking, insurance, fintech support. Boosts providers with numeric and entity recall. |
| `legal` | Depositions, contract review, legal intake. Boosts providers tuned for formal English and proper-noun density. |

Vertical does not change the request shape — it changes which providers/models score highest. A `healthcare` STT call may route to a model with medical-corpus fine-tuning even when a generalist model is cheaper.

## Picking a vertical

Use the most specific applicable vertical. A general intent will route to a generalist model even when a domain-tuned one would be better for your traffic.

If your traffic is mixed, segment at request time — set `vertical` per call based on the user, route, or session metadata. Speko pays no penalty for varying vertical across calls; each call routes on its own.

## Compliance vs vertical

Vertical biases provider scores. It does **not** restrict providers to BAA-covered ones, EU-resident ones, etc. Use [constraints](/concepts/routing#constraints) for compliance-driven allow-lists.
